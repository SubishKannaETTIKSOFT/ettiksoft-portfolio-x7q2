Portfolio · Assessment · Outputs

Everything you need to run a product portfolio.

Capture TRL, MRL, and commercialization status for every product. Generate portfolio dashboards, heatmaps, and board-ready decks — all from one place, all offline.

Quick start: Run python3 -m http.server 8765 in the roadmap-builder folder, open http://localhost:8765, paste seed-test-data.js into the browser console, then explore from the home page.
01

What this tool does

Roadmap Builder is a pure-static, offline web app for product teams. No backend, no install, no accounts. Data lives in your browser's localStorage.

Three layers of work

  • Portfolio — a named collection of products with a strategic context (hero headline, bets, KPIs, quarterly themes, open decisions, risks).
  • Assessment — per-product data: Technology Readiness Level (TRL), Maturity (MRL or Operational Readiness), Commercialization stage, RAG status, compliance, milestones, risks.
  • Outputs — three board-facing surfaces generated live from your data: a visual roadmap, a TRL x Commercialization heatmap, and a printable deck.
TipAll data is local. Nothing leaves your machine. You can export any page as a self-contained HTML file to share or print.
02

The 7 pages

Each page is a self-contained HTML file. Open them in any order.

index.html — Home

Lists every portfolio you have saved. Create, duplicate, or delete portfolios here. Click a portfolio card to open its dashboard.

dashboard.html — Portfolio dashboard

Overview of one portfolio: strategic context summary, a card for every product showing TRL band, RAG status, maturity, and next milestone. Navigate from here to all other views. Use + Add Product to create a new product assessment.

product.html — Product assessment

The per-product editor. Ten sections: Identity, Technology Readiness (TRL ladder), Maturity (MRL or Operational Readiness), Commercialization and Business Stage, Investment and RAG, Next Milestone, Compliance and Standards, Customer Evidence, Product Roadmap, Product Risks. Autosaves as you type.

heatmap.html — TRL x Commercialization heatmap

Plots all products as dots on a 9-row x 5-column grid. Dot size = FTE investment, dot colour = RAG. Use in board meetings to show portfolio distribution. Hover for a detail tooltip; click to open the product assessment. Products without TRL or stage appear in a bench strip below the grid.

deck.html — Board deck

A single-page printable summary: Portfolio Pulse KPIs, Strategic Thesis, Product Status table, Compliance Snapshot (all 6 frameworks), Open Decisions, Top Risks, Next 90 Days milestones, footer. Print with Ctrl+P or download as a self-contained HTML file via Download HTML.

editor.html — Strategic context editor

Edits the portfolio-level strategic context: Masthead, Hero, KPIs, Bets, Actions, Quarterly themes, Open Decisions, Risks. Does not edit products — use product.html for that.

output.html — Visual roadmap

The one-page visual roadmap output. Renders the strategic context into a print-ready layout. Download as HTML or print directly from the browser.

03

End-to-end walkthrough

Follow these steps to go from a blank browser to a board-ready deck.

Step 1 — Start the server

$ cd roadmap-builder
$ python3 -m http.server 8765
Serving HTTP on 0.0.0.0 port 8765 ...

Open http://localhost:8765 in your browser.

Step 2 — Create a portfolio

On the home page, click + Create new portfolio. Give it a name (e.g. "Q3 Product Portfolio"). This opens the strategic context editor automatically.

Step 3 — Fill the strategic context

In editor.html, fill in: Masthead (company name, date, version), Hero headline and accent word, 3 Bets, quarterly themes, and any open decisions or risks. Everything autosaves. When done, click Back to Dashboard.

Step 4 — Add products

On the dashboard, click + Add Product for each product in your portfolio. You are taken to product.html for each one.

Step 5 — Assess each product

For each product, fill in: Identity (name, owner, one-liner), TRL (click the ladder — be honest and conservative, TRL is retrospective), Maturity level, Commercialization stage, Investment (FTE count, budget tier), RAG status and reasoning, and Next Milestone. Add compliance states for the frameworks that apply. Everything autosaves as you type.

Step 6 — Review the dashboard

Return to dashboard.html. Each product card now shows its TRL band, RAG dot, maturity chip, and next milestone date. The strategic context summary shows your hero headline and bets.

Step 7 — Open the heatmap

Click View Heatmap in the topbar. All products appear as dots. The goal over time: move products up (higher TRL) and to the right (higher commercialization stage). Hover over any dot for a detail card.

Step 8 — Generate the deck

Click Generate Deck. Review all 9 sections. When ready for the board, click Download HTML to save a self-contained offline file, or Print to go straight to PDF via the browser print dialog.

NoteThe deck, heatmap, and roadmap all read live from localStorage. Any edits you make in the product or editor pages are immediately reflected — no manual refresh needed.
04

TRL reference card

Technology Readiness Level (TRL) is a retrospective scale — only assign a level based on what has already been demonstrated, not what you plan to do.

  • TRL 1 — Basic principles observed. Scientific idea, no experimental proof.
  • TRL 2 — Technology concept formulated. Application of basic principles identified.
  • TRL 3 — Experimental proof of concept. Lab experiments confirm feasibility.
  • TRL 4 — Technology validated in lab. Component or system validated in controlled environment.
  • TRL 5 — Technology validated in relevant environment. Performance validated in environment representative of real-world use.
  • TRL 6 — Technology demonstrated in relevant environment. Prototype demonstrated in operational environment.
  • TRL 7 — System prototype demonstration. Full prototype demonstrated in operational environment.
  • TRL 8 — System complete and qualified. Technology proven to work in its final form and under expected conditions.
  • TRL 9 — Actual system proven in operational environment. Ready for full-rate production.
Heatmap bandsTRL 1-3 = Research (taupe), TRL 4-6 = Development (sage), TRL 7-9 = Deployment (marine). The heatmap colours your dot accordingly.
05

Maturity levels

Maturity is assessed differently depending on the product type. Switch the type selector in the product form and the maturity section adapts automatically.

Hardware — Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL)

A 1-10 scale measuring how ready a technology is to be manufactured at the required rate and quality:

  • MRL 1-3 — Manufacturing feasibility assessed; basic manufacturing implications identified.
  • MRL 4-5 — Manufacturing processes identified; capability demonstrated in lab.
  • MRL 6-7 — Prototype manufacturing demonstrated; production-representative processes in place.
  • MRL 8-9 — Pilot manufacturing line qualified; low-rate initial production demonstrated.
  • MRL 10 — Full-rate production demonstrated; lean production practices established.

Software — Operational Readiness (1-5)

  • 1 — Concept — Single-developer prototype; no SLA capability.
  • 2 — Pilot — Internal or limited external use; manual deployment.
  • 3 — Limited — Small number of production customers; on-call runbook exists.
  • 4 — Production — Stable production deployment; SLAs met; automated CI/CD.
  • 5 — Scaled — Multi-region, high-availability, self-healing infrastructure.

Service — Not assessed

For service-type products the maturity section is hidden. Services are assessed primarily through commercialization stage and business stage instead.

06

Commercialization stages

The 5-stage commercialization model describes how far along a product is from idea to market.

  • Stage 1 — Identification — Market opportunity identified; problem defined; customer discovery underway. No product yet.
  • Stage 2 — Planning — Business case defined; IP strategy in place; first prototype or proof-of-concept in progress.
  • Stage 3 — Design and Build — Functional prototype built; design-partners or pilot customers engaged; go-to-market planned.
  • Stage 4 — Validation — Beta product in use by paying or pilot customers; product-market fit being tested; feedback loop active.
  • Stage 5 — Sales and Production — General availability; active sales motion; production or deployment at scale; revenue generating.

On the heatmap, the X-axis represents these five stages. The goal is to move products right over successive quarters.

07

Compliance frameworks

The product compliance section tracks six named frameworks plus unlimited custom entries. States: n/a / not started / in progress / aligned / certified.

ISO 21434 — Road vehicle cybersecurity

International standard for cybersecurity engineering in road vehicles. Mandatory for OEM supply chains targeting EU, UK, and many APAC markets. Covers threat analysis (TARA), cybersecurity management systems, and secure development lifecycle.

AIS-189 — Indian automotive cybersecurity

India's Automotive Industry Standard for cybersecurity in vehicles, aligned with ISO 21434 and UNECE R155. Required for type-approval of connected vehicles in India from 2024 onwards.

UNECE R155 — UN regulation on vehicle cybersecurity

United Nations Economic Commission for Europe Regulation 155. Mandatory vehicle type-approval requirement in the EU, UK, Japan, and other adopting regions. Requires a certified Cybersecurity Management System (CSMS) from OEM and supply chain.

CERT-IN — Indian Computer Emergency Response Team

India's national cybersecurity agency. Products handling Indian user data or operating critical infrastructure must comply with CERT-IN's mandatory incident reporting and cybersecurity practices (2022 directive).

ISO 27001 — Information security management

The international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Certification demonstrates a systematic approach to managing sensitive data. Widely required by enterprise customers and financial sector buyers as a procurement prerequisite.

TISAX — Trusted Information Security Assessment Exchange

Automotive industry-specific information security assessment scheme managed by the ENX Association. Required by most German OEMs (VW, BMW, Mercedes) and their Tier-1 suppliers for handling sensitive project data. Based on the VDA ISA questionnaire, which is ISO 27001-aligned.

Custom frameworksUse the Custom standards row in the product compliance section to add any additional frameworks (e.g. SOC 2 Type II, GDPR Article 32, Common Criteria EAL4+).
08

Backup and restore

All data is stored in your browser's localStorage. It is specific to the browser, device, and origin (http://localhost:8765) you use. Clearing your browser data will delete it.

Export your data

Open the browser DevTools console (F12 then Console) and run:

> copy(localStorage.getItem('roadmap-builder.portfolios'))

This copies the raw JSON to your clipboard. Paste it into a .json file and save it somewhere safe.

Restore your data

Open the DevTools console and run, replacing PASTE_JSON_HERE with the contents of your backup file:

> localStorage.setItem('roadmap-builder.portfolios', 'PASTE_JSON_HERE')
> location.reload()

Move to a different browser or device

Export as above, then restore on the new device. Remember to use the same local server address (http://localhost:8765) — localStorage is origin-scoped.

Download individual pages as offline files

The Download HTML button on deck.html and output.html exports a fully self-contained, data-embedded HTML file. This file can be emailed, archived, or opened in any browser without a server. It reflects the data at the moment of download — it does not stay live.

WarningLegacy data (if you used an older version of this tool) is stored under roadmap-builder.roadmaps. It is automatically migrated to roadmap-builder.portfolios on first load. A backup copy is kept under roadmap-builder.legacy-backup.
